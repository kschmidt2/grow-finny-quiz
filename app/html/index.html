2. If you fall into the 22% tax bracket, what percentage of your income will you pay in federal income taxes?
  • Less than 22%
  • Exactly 22%
  • More than 22%
That's correct.
That's incorrect.

The U.S. has a progressive tax system, which means that people with higher incomes pay higher federal income tax rates. However, the amount of tax you pay isn’t dictated by the tax bracket where your income falls, but rather the rates leading up to and including that rate.

For instance, if your taxable income was $50,000 in 2020, you’d be taxed at a rate of 10% on the first $9,875, 12% on the next $30,250, and 22% on the last $9,875. This would make your effective tax rate, or the amount you actually pay, 13.6%.

4. True or false: Unemployment benefits are subject to federal income tax.
  • True
  • False
That's correct.
That's incorrect.

Generally, unemployment benefits are subject to federal income tax. However, the American Rescue Plan waives federal tax on unemployment benefits worth up to $10,200 for the year 2020. Any benefits received over that amount are still subject to federal income taxes, and depending on where you live, you may also need to pay state or local taxes on that income.
